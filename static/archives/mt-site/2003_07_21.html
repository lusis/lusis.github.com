John Gilmore is at it again

John Gilmore is raising more hell

I've been following John Gilmore for a while. Despite my desire wanting to smack him around for not knowing the difference between a democracy and a federal republic, I was really interested in his case against the government that requires a picture ID to fly nowadays. At one point, I printed out the entire brief and took it with me on a trip to Michigan hoping to get the Huffman family lawyer to look at it.

I got my email from Politech over the weekend. Michelle and I both were a bit flabergasted when I finished reading her the story.

I think the whole thing is just stupid. I also think British Airways might want to retrain the flight crew on the stupidity of thinking that security screener would give a badge to a "suspected terrorist" to identify him.

John Gilmore is actually making a bigger statement with this. Were his civil rights violated? Not in the least. The real key lies in what he says after discussing things with a BA rep:

"
I asked whether I would be permitted to fly if I wore other buttons,
perhaps one saying "Hooray for Tony Blair". She said she thought that
would be OK.
"

People like Sean Hannity, like to rail against opposing speech saying that it's unpatriotic to question authority in a time of war or that it crosses a line at some point. Of ocurse only the active political party can tell you what that line is but let's ignore that.

I also like this part:

"
I told her that it refers to all of us, everyone, being
suspected of being terrorists, being searched without cause, being
queued in lines and pens, forced to take our shoes off, to identify
ourselves, to drink our own breast milk, to submit to indignities.
Everyone is a suspected terrorist in today's America, including all
the innocent people, and that's wrong.
"

Thanks to political correctness, we aren't allowed to profile criminals because it's racism we are left with the above situation.
